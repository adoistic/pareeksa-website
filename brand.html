Brand guidelines

The instrument
of fair judgment.

Pareeksa leads with one mark — a fountain-pen nib, the instrument of the examiner. This page is how to use it: the logo and its variants, clear space and sizing, colour, type and voice. Everything here is free to download and use when you represent Pareeksa.

The mark

A nib, not a checkmark

The symbol is a fountain-pen nib seen head-on — the tool a marker uses to make a judgment. It carries authority without cliché. It is bilaterally symmetric; the breather hole and slit are real negative space, never filled. We lead with the symbol; the wordmark joins it only when the name is needed.

Lockups

Mark plus wordmark

When the name is needed, pair the nib with the wordmark — "Pareeksa" set in Geist Semibold, tracking −0.02em, fixed case. The horizontal lockup is primary; the stacked lockup suits square and cover placements. The wordmark never appears without the symbol, and never leads it.

Clear space & sizing

Give it room

Keep a margin of clear space around the mark equal to the diameter of its breather hole. Don't crowd it with type, edges or other logos.

Clear space — at least one breather-hole diameter on every side.

Colour

One accent, used with restraint

Colour means status, action or a mark — never decoration. Institutional blue is the single accent. Marking red is reserved for a marker's annotations and appears nowhere in the interface.

Click a swatch to copy its hex.

Typography

One engineered family

Geist does the talking; Geist Mono carries every number. Monospace numerals are the precision signature — use them for scores, IDs, totals and timestamps. Build hierarchy with weight and size, never by mixing families.

Voice

Precise, calm, accountable

Write like serious infrastructure for high-stakes work. Say what a thing is, its status, and the next action. Short sentences, concrete nouns, active voice, exact numbers in mono. Reassure under stress — the system is on the user's side and their work is safe.

Avoid hype: no "seamless", "powerful", "revolutionary", "robust", "comprehensive". Lead with the point; close with what the reader can now do.
